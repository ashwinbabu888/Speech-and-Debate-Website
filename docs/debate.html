Speech & Debate

Debate Events

Public Forum

Public Forum is a prepared debate event in which you read a structured argument case and give 3 more speeches where you refute your opponents' arguments while defending your own. There are 2 competitors per team and they each read 2 speeches per round.

Parliamentary

Parliamentary is a spontaneous event similar to Public Forum in which you create a case on the spot with limited time. You must read it, refute your opponents' arguments, and defend your own. Also like in Public Forum, there are 2 competitors per team that each take turns reading speeches.

Lincoln-Douglas

Lincoln-Douglas is a prepared debate event in which you read a structured argument case and must refute your opponents' arguments while defending your own. Unlike the other events, the content of Lincoln-Douglas is focused on morality and philosophy as opposed to evidence-based debate. Additionally, this is a single-competitor event.

Speech Events

Original Oratory: A speech event in which a student chooses a topic and delivers a speech about it. Focused on argumentative reasoning with logic, rhetoric, and evidence.

Expository: A speech event in which a student creates as original speech that describes, clarifies, or illustrates an object, idea, concept, or process. Focused on research and information.

Dramatic Interpretation: A speech event in which a student recreates a piece of literature without props or costumes. It focuses on character development, depth, and emotion.

Humorous Interpretation: A speech event in which a student delivers a section of a published work. Focused on comedic skill, delivery/timing, and character development.

Duo Interpretation: A speech event in which 2 students deliver a performance of a published play or story. No props or costumes are allowed, and the focus is on emotion and the story environment.

Program Oral Interpretation: A speech event in which a student gives a 10 minute performance about a central theme. Focused on integrating multiple types of literature into a single cohesive performance.

Impromptu: A speech event in which a student gets 7 minutes to choose a topic and deliver a speech on it. Topics can range from current events to celebrities. Focused on structure.

National Extemporaneous: A speech event in which a student must use prepared evidence and research to spontaneously deliver a speech regarding 1 of 3 questions about national issues.

International Extemporaneous: A speech event in which a student must use prepared evidence and research to spontaneously deliver a speech regarding 1 of 3 questions about international issues.
